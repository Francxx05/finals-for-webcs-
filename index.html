About the Story

A quiet story about grief, healing, and love. Bloom follows two women whose lives meet when they need it most.

Kara, born into privilege, carries the weight of her family’s expectations. As the heir to a powerful company, she hides her true self behind perfection, bound by duty and fear of disappointing those around her.

Naomi, a college graduate adrift in uncertainty, struggles with regret and the growing distance between her dreams and reality.

When their paths cross, both find comfort in the other’s presence. But as their connection deepens, they’re forced to face the truths they’ve long avoided and discover if love can still bloom in the shadow of grief.

Gameplay Preview

Bloom Into You is planned to be developed using Unreal Engine, aiming to deliver cinematic storytelling and emotional realism through detailed environments, expressive animations, and immersive lighting.

Inspired by narrative-driven titles such as Life is Strange and Detroit: Become Human, the game will focus on player choices, emotional dialogue, and branching storylines. Every decision will shape how Kara and Naomi’s story unfolds influencing relationships, key events, and the ultimate fate of both characters.

The game will feature multiple endings, including a Normal Ending, a True Ending, and a Bad Ending, allowing players to experience different outcomes based on the choices they make.

About the Creator

Hello! I’m Francheska Rivera, also known online as “Juno.”

My passion for games began when I was ten years old, inspired by the Final Fantasy series created by Square Enix. Their storytelling and artistry fueled my love for creating and sparked my fascination with computers, art, and game development.

My path hasn’t been a straight one — I’ve been a STEM student, tried architecture, and then shifted after one semester. But through all the uncertainty, one thing stayed constant: my desire to create games and stories that feel alive.

That dream eventually became Bloom Into You, a project I began in 2021. It started as an idea for an art portfolio but grew into something more personal — a story that reflects my own journey of doubt, growth, and love. To bring it to life, I formed a small creative team called MisoSheep, dedicated to turning Bloom into a heartfelt experience others can connect with.
